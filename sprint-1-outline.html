Course Outline & Lesson Plan

A high-impact, 60-minute intensive designed to move STEM researchers and founders from "Lab-Bench Theory" to "Investable Narrative."

Learning Objectives

By the end of this module, participants will:

  • Deconstruct the economic and regulatory barriers specific to ASEAN deeptech ventures (The "Valley of Death").
  • Explore First Principles Thinking to validate technical moats beyond just IP.
  • Examine the Mission Requirements Matrix for prioritizing MVP subsystems.

Learning Outcomes

By the end of the session, learners will be able to:

  • Articulate a "Bifocal Strategy" that aligns a Moonshot vision with immediate, grant-fundable milestones.
  • Construct a defensible Tech Moat strategy that integrates regulatory complexity.
  • Draft a prioritized MVP roadmap that filters "nice-to-have" engineering from "funding-critical" milestones.

Detailed Module Breakdown

01 10 Mins

The Context: Escaping the Lab

Pain Point Addressed: The "Grant Rejection Cycle". Why technical novelty alone fails to secure commercial funding in ASEAN.

Approach

  • Shock Therapy: Contrasting a "Science Project" vs. a "Venture Asset".
  • The Bifocal Framework: How to sell the Moonshot (Vision) while building the Ladder (Product).
02 25 Mins

Designing for Complexity (Moats)

Pain Point Addressed: "Google will just copy us." Fear of zero defensibility once the product launches.

Approach

  • Systems Thinking: Mapping Tech, Regulation, and Economics as a single system.
  • Case Study Deconstruction: Anatomy of a failed unicorn (Theranos) vs. a success (SpaceX).
03 25 Mins

Strategic Engineering (The MVP)

Pain Point Addressed: Running out of money before proving viability. Building "cool features" that investors don't care about.

Approach

  • Mission Matrix: Differentiating Mission Objectives (Ends) from Requirements (Means).
  • Live Mapping: Participants apply the "Build vs. Buy vs. Simulate" filter to their own tech stack.

Facilitation & Engagement Plan

Pre-Event (Context Setting)

Goal: Prime the cognitive load so the 60 minutes are pure application.

  • Distribution: "The Deeptech Primer" PDF (Read time: 5 mins).
  • Input: Participants submit their "One Big Blocker" via form.

During Event (The Sprint)

Goal: High-velocity learning with zero fluff.

  • 00-10m: Framing & "Shock Therapy" (Socratic questioning on current failures).
  • 10-35m: Systems Thinking (Live case study dissection).
  • 35-60m: Strategic Engineering (Interactive mapping of their own MVP).

Post-Event (Application)

Goal: Conversion to actionable roadmap.

  • Artifacts: Digital Deeptech Canvas template & Session Recording.
  • Office Hours: Exclusive link for 1:1 Strategic Consultation (Paid) for deep-dive.
