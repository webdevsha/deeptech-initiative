Deeptech Founder’s Blueprint

Sprint Course Outline

A 60-minute intensive using Adult Participatory Learning to diagnose your venture's investability gap. This is not a lecture; it is a strategic intervention using proprietary visual frameworks.

Trainer

Razlan Dhamir Hamdan (Deeptech Operator)

Methodology

Advanced Bloom’s Taxonomy (Analyze, Evaluate, Create)

Target Audience

STEM Researchers, Pre-Seed Founders, Grant Seekers

Critical Output

A "Green-Light" Roadmap for Funding & MVP Development

01

Strategic Positioning of Technology

Outcome: Strong Positioning & Compelling Narrative

The Context: Science vs. Venture (8 Mins)

Theory: Deeptech has historically been "State-Sponsored" before "Venture-Backed". We analyze the funding origins of mega-projects to understand the narrative required.

Historical Case Studies:
  • NASA Apollo (1960s): Funded for geopolitical dominance, not science. Source: Mission Economy.
  • Silicon Valley (Fairchild): Early chips funded by Minuteman missile contracts. Source: Steve Blank.

Framework: The Moat Radar (10 Mins)

Deep Dive: Analysis of Technical Moats vs. Regulatory Moats. How to position your "Research Gap" as a "Market Gap".

  • Case Study: How Aphelia used lunar regulations to create a monopoly before building the product.

Active Exercise: Deeptech Positioning (7 Mins)

Application: Participants map their venture on the canvas live.

Canvas adapted for Malaysia from MIT Sloan: The Incumbent's Deep Tech Strategy Playbook.

Peer Review & Synthesis (5 Mins)

Gap Awareness: "Did you struggle to fill the 'Regulatory Defense' quadrant? This is the most common reason for grant rejection. We address this specifically in our 1:1 Technical Review."
02

MVP Development Strategy for Funding

Outcome: A Tech Development Plan that Wins Commercial Objectives

Framework: The Funding Bridge (8 Mins)

Theory: Mapping engineering milestones directly to funding tranches. Don't build what you can't fund.

  • Concept: Mission Objectives (Ends) vs. Mission Requirements (Means).
  • First Principles Economics: Proving unit economics on paper before prototyping.

Active Exercise: Mission Requirements Matrix (14 Mins)

Application: We use the "Red Pen" method on your feature list to ruthlessly categorize engineering tasks.

Visual Tool: The Prioritization Matrix
Column A: Funding Milestone Column B: Critical Subsystems (Build) Column C: Distractions (Buy/Simulate)
Example: Series A
"Prove flight capability"
  • Propulsion Core
  • Guidance Software
  • Custom Chassis (Buy off-shelf)
  • User Dashboard (Simulate)
Tactical Output: A pruned, lean MVP roadmap that fits a RM 500k grant budget.

Review & Synthesis (8 Mins)

Closing: How to translate Columns A & B into the "Gantt Chart" required by MOSTI/Cradle applications.

The Upsell Trigger: "You have the strategy. If you need someone to write the complex technical feasibility papers to prove 'Column B' works, book our Technical Document Consult."

By the End of 60 Minutes:

You Will Have Analyzed:

  • Why your current narrative fails the "Investor/Grant" test.
  • The gap between your scientific innovation and commercial reality.

You Will Have Created:

  • A 1-Page "Sovereign Positioning Statement" (using the Canvas).
  • A prioritized "Kill List" of features to cut from your MVP (using the Matrix).
  • A validated "Tech Moat" strategy.
